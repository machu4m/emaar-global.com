Important — understand what offshore means
A UAE offshore company cannot conduct business inside the UAE, cannot lease office space in the UAE, and does not entitle the owner to a UAE residency visa. It is designed for international business, holding structures, and asset protection. If you want to operate inside the UAE, you need a Mainland or Free Zone company instead.
What Is It

What Exactly Is a UAE Offshore Company?

A UAE offshore company is a legal entity registered in the UAE — under jurisdictions like RAK ICC or JAFZA — but specifically designed to operate outside the UAE. It's not a shell company. It's a fully legitimate corporate structure widely used by multinationals, HNWIs, and global entrepreneurs.

The UAE's offshore jurisdictions are internationally recognised and compliant with OECD standards. Unlike some offshore destinations, UAE offshore companies carry genuine credibility with banks and counterparties globally.

The most common uses are holding shares in other companies, holding real estate (including UAE property), managing intellectual property, international trading, and structuring investments across multiple jurisdictions.

✓ Offshore is right for you if:
  • ✓You operate internationally and don't need UAE presence
  • ✓You want to hold assets (real estate, shares, IP) in a tax-efficient structure
  • ✓You need a holding company above your operating entities
  • ✓You want to reduce complexity and cost vs mainland/freezone
✗ Offshore is NOT right for you if:
  • ✗You want to sell to UAE customers or the UAE government
  • ✗You need a UAE residency visa
  • ✗You want to rent an office or hire staff in the UAE
  • ✗You need a company that appears as a Dubai local address

Key Advantages

What You Get with a UAE Offshore Company

Legitimate, internationally respected, and cost-effective. Here are the six main reasons to choose a UAE offshore structure.

0% Tax — All Income

No corporate tax, no capital gains tax, no withholding tax, no personal income tax. All profit remains entirely with the company — the UAE imposes no tax on offshore entities' income.

Strong Asset Protection

Your assets are held in a separate legal entity. In the event of personal legal disputes, bankruptcy, or business creditor claims, assets held in the offshore company remain protected behind the corporate veil.

International Credibility

The UAE is a globally respected jurisdiction with strong anti-money laundering compliance. Unlike some offshore centres, a UAE offshore company is accepted by major banks, institutions, and partners worldwide.

No Office Required

Offshore companies have no requirement for physical premises. You don't need to rent or maintain any UAE office space, keeping your annual operating costs minimal — often just the license renewal fee.

Full Profit Repatriation

Transfer funds freely to any country with no restrictions, no approval requirements, and no capital controls. The UAE has no exchange controls — you move money on your terms.

Low Annual Running Cost

After setup, the main recurring cost is your annual license renewal — typically AED 5,000–8,000 per year. No office, no staff requirements, no audit obligation for most structures. One of the lowest-cost offshore jurisdictions globally.
